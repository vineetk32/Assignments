the game underwent major development in the 18 century and became the national sport of england betting played a major part in that development with rich patrons forming their own select elevens cricket was prominent in london as early as 1707 and large crowds flocked to matches on the artillery ground in finsbury the single wicket form of the sport attracted huge crowds and wagers to match bowling evolved around 1760 when bowlers began to pitch the ball instead of rolling or skimming it towards the batsman this caused a revolution in bat design because to deal with the bouncing ball it was necessary to introduce the modern straight bat in place of the old hockey stick shape the hambledon club was founded in the 1760 and for the next 20 years until the formation of marylebone cricket club and the opening of lords old ground in 1787 hambledon was both the games greatest club and its focal point mcc quickly became the sports premier club and the custodian of the laws of cricket new laws introduced in the latter part of the 18 century included the three stump wicket and leg before wicket lbw
